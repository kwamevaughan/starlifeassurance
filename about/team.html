Incorporated as a limited liability company in compliance with the new Insurance Law 2006, Act 724 in October 2005, thus making it the first Life Assurance company to be separated as a composite Company.

Mrs. Boatemaa Kakra Duffuor-Nyarko
Board Member & C.E.O

Boatemaa Kakra Duffuor-Nyarko is the Chief Executive Officer of StarLife Assurance and a qualified Fellow of the Institute and Faculty of Actuaries (UK). She holds a first-class BSc (Hons) in Economics from the University of Warwick, a Postgraduate Diploma in Actuarial Science with distinction, and an MSc in Actuarial Management from Cass Business School, London. An alumna of Ghana International School, her strong academic foundation has shaped her successful career in insurance and actuarial practice.

Before becoming CEO, she served as Executive Director and Chief Actuary at StarLife. Earlier, she worked with QED Actuaries and Consultants (Aon Hewitt) in South Africa, where she provided actuarial consulting services to clients across Ghana and several African countries including Kenya, Zambia, Malawi, Mozambique, and Mauritius.

Her expertise spans valuations, premium rating, embedded values, surplus analysis, and product design. At StarLife, she has played a pivotal role in developing innovative solutions such as the Family Protection Plan, Child Lifeline Plus, and CashBuilder Plus, as well as enhancing the competitiveness of group products.

Kakra is widely respected for her leadership, technical excellence, and commitment to building inclusive, client-focused life assurance solutions for individuals, families, and businesses.

Bernice Quainoo
Chief Operations Officer

Bernice Quainoo is the Chief Operations Officer of StarLife Assurance, bringing more than two decades of rich experience in Ghana's insurance industry. A Chartered Insurer and Associate of the Chartered Insurance Institute (ACII, UK), she also holds a Diploma in Insurance from the West African Insurance Institute (WAII).

Before joining StarLife, Bernice built her career at Star Assurance Company Ltd, where she excelled in various roles across marketing, customer service, corporate business, and underwriting. Her diverse experience has given her a deep appreciation of both client needs and operational efficiency.

Bernice's academic background is equally strong. She holds a Master of Business Administration in Project Management from the Ghana Institute of Management and Public Administration (GIMPA) and a Bachelor of Arts (Hons) degree in English from the University of Cape Coast.

With her blend of professional expertise and people-centered leadership, Bernice continues to play a vital role in strengthening StarLife's operations. She is dedicated to ensuring that the company delivers reliable, efficient, and customer-friendly services, making life assurance more accessible and valuable to families and businesses across Ghana.

Eric Gyebi
Chief Finance and Strategy Officer

Eric Gyebi is an accomplished finance and insurance professional with nearly two decades of leadership experience in Ghana's life insurance sector. As Chief Finance and Strategy Officer of StarLife Assurance, he oversees a broad portfolio that includes strategy, finance, distribution, premium collections, administration, training, data analytics, and overall business performance.

Known for his passion for growth and efficiency, Eric is credited with transforming StarLife's finance function into a strategic powerhouse, playing a key role in positioning the company as one of the largest and most efficient insurers in Ghana.

Eric is a Chartered Accountant, Chartered Insurer, and Chartered Tax Consultant. He holds a Bachelor of Science in Administration (Accounting) and an MBA in Finance from the University of Ghana Business School. He is also a Fellow of the Institute of Chartered Accountants (Ghana), and a member of both the Chartered Insurance Institute (UK) and the Chartered Institute of Taxation (Ghana).

With expertise spanning finance, investment, management, and insurance, Eric brings both technical depth and strategic vision to his role. He remains committed to driving excellence and delivering value to clients, partners, and stakeholders of StarLife Assurance.

Rosemary Aryee is the Head of Human Resources and Administration at StarLife Assurance, where she has contributed to the company's growth for more than thirteen years. Mr. Peter  Kyei Nimoh
Head of Individual Distribution

Peter Kyei Nimoh is a seasoned insurance professional with over 18 years of experience in Ghana's insurance industry. A Chartered Insurer (ACII) from the Chartered Insurance Institute in the UK, he also holds both Diploma and Advanced Diploma qualifications from the Ghana Insurance College, along with a BA (Hons.) degree in Political Science from the University of Ghana.

Peter began his career with Global Impact Insurance Brokers, before moving to Unique Insurance Company and later Enterprise Life Assurance, where he gained valuable experience across key areas of the business. In 2007, he joined StarLife Assurance as Zonal Manager for the Western and Central Regions. His outstanding performance earned him elevation to oversee the Southern Sector, further strengthening StarLife's presence in the region.

Over the years, he has held several leadership roles within the company, including Head of Retail Business and Head of Sales Training and Agency Development. His career reflects a strong commitment to growth, professionalism, and service excellence.

With his deep industry knowledge and proven leadership, Peter continues to play a key role in driving StarLife's mission of delivering trusted life assurance solutions to individuals and families across Ghana.

Mr. Frank Oppong-Yeboah
Board Chairman

Frank Oppong-Yeboah is the Board Chairman of StarLife Assurance and a distinguished leader in Ghana.s insurance industry. Before assuming this role, he served as Executive Vice Chairman of the company.

A Chartered Insurer and Fellow of the Chartered Insurance Institute (UK), Frank is also a graduate of the University of Ghana. In 1999, he earned the prestigious Rutter Medal for being the best qualifying fellow of the Chartered Insurance Institute, a recognition of his excellence in the field.

With more than 26 years of experience, he has worked extensively across technical and management roles. He began his career at the State Insurance Company Ltd., rising through the Marine and Motor Departments before moving to Star Assurance Co. Ltd., where he served as Chief Manager (Technical Operations) and later General Manager.

Frank has enriched his expertise through advanced training at international institutions in Switzerland and the UK. He also serves as a visiting lecturer at the West African Insurance Institute in The Gambia, helping shape the next generation of insurance professionals.

Beyond StarLife, he chairs the board of UniCredit Ghana Limited, reflecting his broader influence in Ghana.s financial sector.

Mrs. Boatemaa Kakra Duffuor-Nyarko 
Board Member

Mrs. Boatemaa Kakra Duffuor-Nyarko is the Chief Executive Officer of StarLife Assurance Company Limited. She holds a BSc. (Hons) first class degree in Economics from University of Warwick (UK), a Postgraduate Diploma in Actuarial Science with distinction as well as an MSc. in Actuarial Management from the prestigious Cass Business School, City University (London, UK). She is a qualified Fellow of the Institute and Faculty of Actuaries in the United Kingdom.

Kakra is an Alumnus of the Ghana International School where she obtained both her ordinary and advanced level certificates, majoring in mathematics, chemistry and biology. Until her appointment as Chief Executive Officer, she was the Executive Director and Chief Actuary of the Company. She had also worked several years with QED Actuaries and Consultants (Aon Hewitt) in South Africa, where she had the opportunity to provide actuarial consulting services for not only South African clients but also those from Zambia, Kenya, Malawi, Mozambique, Namibia, Mauritius and Ghana.

She has had wide exposure in her field including specialization in performing Valuations (Gross Premium and
Net Premium), Premium Rating, Embedded Values, Appraisal Values, Analysis of Surplus and Projected Accounts. Therefore, she has the requisite experience in Actuarial Management. Mrs. Duffuor-Nyarko has been working in the insurance industry for the over nine years and has worked in various departments such as marketing, customer service, underwriting, accounts and actuarial departments.

She has contributed immensely to new product design for diversified markets and customers. Boatemaa played
a significant role in the design and development of the company’s recently launched 3 life solutions (Family Protection Plan, Child Lifeline Plus and CashBuilder Plus). StarLife’s group products (Employee Security Plan, Mortgage Protection Plan, Credit Life Schemes and Group Welfare Schemes) have also enjoyed competitive
rating as a result of the high pedigree that the company’s Chief Actuary and Executive Director brings to the
table. She has attended several courses and business workshops internationally and locally.

Mr. Kofi Duffuor
Board Member

Kofi Duffuor is the Group Chief Executive Officer of Star Assurance Group Limited and a seasoned leader in Ghana’s insurance industry. Previously, he served as Chief Executive Officer of Star Assurance Company Ltd., where through dedication and strategic leadership, he and his team moved the company from near the bottom of the industry to become the third-largest non-life insurer in Ghana. Under his leadership, Star Assurance twice won the Chartered Institute of Marketing Ghana (CIMG) Insurance Company of the Year award.

In March 2011, Kofi was unanimously appointed Chairman of the Board of WAICA Reinsurance Corporation PLC, a multinational reinsurer headquartered in Sierra Leone, where he continues to provide strong leadership. He has also served on the executive of the Ghana Insurers Association, chairing its Finance and Secretariat Committee.

Kofi’s leadership has been widely recognized—he was named Best CEO of the Year 2018 in the General Insurance Category by the CEOs Network Ghana, and he is also a recipient of the Ghana Business Leaders Excellence Gold Award.

He holds an MBA in Entrepreneurial Management from the University of Ghana, is a Chartered Insurer, and a Fellow of both the Chartered Insurance Institute (UK) and the Chartered Insurance Institute of Ghana.

Mr. Tutu Kwachie
Board Member

Tutu Kwachie is an achievement-oriented investment banking professional with extensive experience in corporate finance, valuation, and strategic advisory across multiple industries. He currently serves as Head of Structured Finance at uniSecurities, where he provides innovative financing solutions and investment guidance.

Previously, he was a Senior Investment Manager at GroFin Africa, managing portfolios and leading initiatives such as restructuring, equity sales, and leverage buyouts. He also consulted for Glico Financial Services, supporting the design and implementation of investment and corporate finance policies.

His international experience includes serving as Regional Director and Senior Associate with Madison Street Capital LLC, a U.S.-based investment banking firm, where he oversaw transactions in Ghana and West Africa. Before that, he headed Analytical Services at Houlihan Capital LLC in Chicago, specializing in valuation advisory, mergers and acquisitions, and financial restructuring.

Earlier in his career, he worked as Unit Head in Corporate Banking at Bank of Africa, Ghana, and as a Cluster Revenue Analyst at Ghana International Airlines.

Tutu holds a BA (Hons) in Economics with Statistics from the University of Ghana, a Postgraduate Diploma in Management Science and E-Commerce from the London School of Commerce, and an MBA in Finance from Charles Sturt University, Australia. He is also a Licensed International Financial Analyst and member of several global professional bodies.

Miss Rebecca Lomo Atswei
Board Member

Rebecca Lomo Atswei is a Chartered Accountant with extensive experience in accounting, auditing, and financial management. Over her career, she has held senior roles with SSNIT, Trust Logistics Ltd, the Internal Revenue Service, Oduru Addiyah & Osei Chartered Accountants, and KPMG.

She currently serves as Board Chair of NTHC Properties and as a Board Member of NDK Financial Services Ltd. She also chairs audit and finance committees under the Public Financial Management Act, including for the Ministry of Aviation and the District Assembly Common Fund, and is a member of the Allied Health Professions Council.

Previously, she was General Manager of Finance and Administration at Trust Logistics Ltd, where she led the turnaround of the business into a self-sustaining company. She also served as Chief Internal Auditor of SSNIT until 2016. In governance, she has been a Board Member and Audit Committee Chair for Republic Bank Ghana Ltd, the Internal Audit Agency, and Trust Logistics Ltd.

Rebecca holds a BSc and an EMBA from the University of Ghana. She is a member of the Institute of Chartered Accountants (Ghana), Association of Certified Fraud Examiners (USA), Institute of Internal Auditors, Institute of Directors (Ghana), and the Chartered Institute of Taxation.

Mr. Eric Osiakwan
Board Member

Eric Osiakwan is a technology entrepreneur and investor with experience spanning 32 African countries. A pioneer in the continent’s digital transformation, he has founded and supported multiple successful tech start-ups while playing a central role in expanding internet infrastructure across Africa.

He established and led the Africa ISP Association for eight years, during which the industry grew by nearly 120%. Eric was also part of the landmark public-private partnership in Kenya that built the TEAMS submarine fiber cable — the first to connect East Africa to the global internet. He later contributed to the rollout of terrestrial fiber networks in West Africa.

Beyond infrastructure, Eric has served as an ICT consultant to leading global institutions including the World Bank, UNDP, USAID, Soros Foundations, and the US Departments of Justice and State, as well as African governments and private sector players. His thought leadership includes co-authoring the globally adopted “Open Access Model” for telecommunications and publishing several works on Africa’s digital economy.

Eric currently serves on the boards of Nyaho Medical Centre, Star Assurance, and Ghana Cyber City. He has held fellowships at MIT, Stanford, and Harvard, reflecting his global recognition as a leader in Africa’s digital growth story.

Mr. Samuel Acquah
Board Member

Samuel Acquah is a seasoned professional with over 20 years of expertise in business management, enterprise risk, portfolio management, and financial analytics. His career spans leadership roles in risk, operations, and strategy across major financial institutions in Africa, Europe, and Asia.

He most recently served as Chief Risk Officer at GCB Bank PLC, where he designed and implemented an Integrated Risk Management Framework to minimize financial loss while supporting revenue growth and sustainable value creation. Earlier, Samuel held senior positions at global banks, including Barclays PLC in London, where he began his career in 2003, and Standard Chartered Bank in Singapore and Dubai. At SCB, he led risk management teams and played a pivotal role in securing Advanced Internal Risk-Based (AIRB) accreditation and driving Basel II implementation across Africa.

In 2009, he joined Ecobank Transnational Incorporated (ETI) as Group Head of Risk Modelling, where he strengthened the bank’s risk management framework across its multinational operations.

Samuel holds a BSc in Geology from the University of Ghana and an MSc in Geoscience Data from Kingston University, London. Beyond work, he enjoys travelling, swimming, football, and spending quality time with his family.
